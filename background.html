ALBERTO

OLEA

A timeline of major milestones...

1995

MIGRATION:
FROM MEXICO TO THE US

The story of my family's migration to the United States has been a significant part of my life. My parents made the difficult decision to leave their home in search of a better life for themselves and their future family. My mother crossed the border while seven months pregnant with me, facing countless dangers and uncertainties along the way. As I grew older, my parents shared their story with me, and I came to understand the immense sacrifice and courage it took for them to make the journey. They faced discrimination and hardship upon arriving in the United States, but they were determined to provide a better life for themselves and their children. Despite the challenges, my family persevered, and I was born in the United States, becoming the first in my family to be a citizen. My parents worked tirelessly to provide for our family, and they instilled in me a strong work ethic and a deep appreciation for the opportunity to continuously learn.

1999

IN CHASE OF OPPORTUNITY:
THE MOVE TO ELGIN

The decision to relocate was driven by my father's job opportunity in the roofing industry, highlighting the role of economic opportunities in shaping human migration patterns. This move also reflects the larger trends of urbanization and suburbanization in the United States, as families seek to find better opportunities and quality of life in different regions.

2004

EDUCATION:
ASSIGNED A LABEL

Tested and placed into the U-46 School Within a School (SWAS) program for academically 'gifted' students.

2013

EDUCATION:
GOING THROUGH THE PUBLIC EDUCATION SYSTEM

The graduation rate of Elgin High School is 78%, which is lower than the Illinois state average of 87%. Currently ranked 2581 out of 3663 Illinois schools, EHS students score 14% proficient in Math and 15% in Reading/Language Arts test scores. Over half of the student population is eligible for free lunch. This is where I went to High School.

2015

MIGRATION:
MOVING TO CHICAGO

Accepted into the University of Illinois at Chicago's Engineering College, I came to the city. As a student in the Industrial Engineering program, I was challenged intellectually and personally. I was surrounded by students from all over the world, each with their own unique stories and backgrounds. I learned to work collaboratively and to think creatively, skills that have served me well throughout my academic and professional life. Attending UIC also gave me the opportunity to reconnect with the city of Chicago, a place that I had always felt a connection to. I explored the neighborhoods and communities that had shaped my parents' experiences, and I was inspired by the diversity and resilience of the city.

2019

HIGHER EDUCATION:
ENGINEERING UNDERGRAD

First in the family to go to college, while pursuing an Industrial Engineering degree: founded Engineers Without Borders UIC Chapter, leader in Sigma Alpha Mu IFC Fraternity, Instititute of Industrial & Systems Engineers representative. Went through a number of internships in downtown Chicago, designed autonomous robotic arms, and made lasting friendships.

2019

ADVOCACY AS A CAREER:
THE DECISION TO PURSUE NON-PROFIT WORK

My decision to pursue a career in non-profit work was influenced by my experiences growing up in a family that had faced significant challenges and obstacles. I sought out organizations that shared my vision, working with groups that focused on issues like affordable housing, healthcare access, and environmental justice. Working in the non-profit world has been challenging, but it has also been incredibly rewarding. I have had the opportunity to work with inspiring individuals and organizations, and to make a tangible impact in the lives of those around me. As I continue to pursue my career in non-profit work, I remain committed to a progressive vision of change, one in which we work together to build a more just and equitable world for all.

2022

HIGHER EDUCATION:
MASSACHUSSETS INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY

As a current student, I am challenged on a daily basis by the rigorous coursework and research projects. The faculty members are world-renowned experts in their fields, and I am constantly learning from their insights and expertise. The resources available to students at MIT are unparalleled, and I have had the opportunity to work with cutting-edge technology and software.

2023

R1 RESEARCH INSTITUTION:
NORTHWESTERN UNIVERSITY

Joining a research lab at Northwestern University was a defining experience in my academic and professional journey. It gave me the tools, skills, and confidence I needed to pursue a career in research, and it cemented my passion for statistics and data science as a means of making a positive impact in the world.
